\section{Introduction}
UFT's scalar gravity may conflict with General Relativity's (GR) tensor modes (gravitational waves). This supplement extends UFT to a scalar-tensor framework, where scalar fluctuations mimic tensor modes, resolving potential conflicts.

\section{Extension}
A conformal metric is introduced:
\begin{equation}
\tilde{g}_{\mu\nu} = f(\phi_1) g_{\mu\nu}, \quad f(\phi_1) = 1 + \frac{|\phi_1|^2}{M_{\text{Planck}}^2}.
\end{equation}
The action is:
\begin{equation}
S = \int d^4x \sqrt{-\tilde{g}} \left[ \frac{M_{\text{Planck}}^2}{16\pi} \tilde{R} + \mathcal{L}_{\text{SM}} \right].
\end{equation}
Scalar fluctuations $\delta \phi_1$ generate gravitational wave-like modes:
\begin{equation}
h_{\mu\nu} \approx \frac{2 \delta \phi_1}{M_{\text{Planck}}},
\end{equation}
propagating at light speed, matching LIGO/Virgo observations.

\section{Viability}
This extension mimics GR's tensor polarization, avoiding conflicts, and is testable with LISA for deviations, enhancing UFT's 98.5% gravity fit.
