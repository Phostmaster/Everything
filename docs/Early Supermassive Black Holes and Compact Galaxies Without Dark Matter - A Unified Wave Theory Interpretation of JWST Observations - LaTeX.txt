\begin{abstract}
The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) has unveiled massive black holes (BHs) and compact, chemically evolved galaxies at redshifts \( z \gtrsim 10 \), challenging \(\Lambda\)CDM with cold dark matter (CDM) and standard baryonic feedback models. Conventional seeding (\(10^2 \, \mathrm{M}_\odot\)) and Eddington-limited growth struggle to explain \(\gtrsim 10^8-10^9 \, \mathrm{M}_\odot\) BHs and \(>10^{10} \, \mathrm{M}_\odot\) stellar systems this early. This study proposes an alternative using Unified Wave Theory (UWT), a two-scalar-field framework with Scalar Boosted Gravity (SBG), initiated by a ``Golden Spark'' phase-splitting at \( t \approx 10^{-36} \, \mathrm{s} \). UWT simulations on a \(128^3\) grid show seed condensates of \(10^4-10^6 \, \mathrm{M}_\odot\) forming at \( z \gtrsim 25 \), growing to \(\gtrsim 10^8-10^9 \, \mathrm{M}_\odot\) by \( z \sim 10 \) via SBG-regulated accretion. A \(32^3\) validation run yielded a single BH (\(1.739 \times 10^{44} \, \mathrm{kg}\)), contrasting a collaborator’s 8 BH result. UWT’s fixed parameters, validated by Bullet Cluster lensing and cosmology, offer a dark matter-free explanation, with testable predictions for JWST and LISA.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Keywords}

\section{Introduction}
JWST observations reveal bright, compact galaxies and candidate supermassive black holes (SMBHs) at \( z \gtrsim 10 \) \citep{Greene2023}, straining \(\Lambda\)CDM timelines for halo assembly and BH growth. Standard models rely on Population III remnants (\(10^2 \, \mathrm{M}_\odot\)) with sustained accretion or direct-collapse seeds (\(10^4-10^6 \, \mathrm{M}_\odot\)) under rare conditions. This paper explores Unified Wave Theory (UWT), where a ``Golden Spark'' at \( t \approx 10^{-36} \, \mathrm{s} \) splits a scalar field into \(\Phi_1\) and \(\Phi_2\), driving structure formation via Scalar Boosted Gravity (SBG) \citep{Khlopov1985}. Using Planck 2016 parameters \citep{Planck2016}, we test UWT on a \(128^3\) grid and a \(32^3\) validation run.

\section{Essentials of Unified Wave Theory (UWT)}
UWT posits two scalar fields (\(\Phi_1\), \(\Phi_2\)) in flat spacetime, unified by a Lagrangian:
\begin{align}
L_\mathrm{ToE} = &\frac{1}{2} \sum_{a=1}^2 (\partial_\mu \Phi_a)^2 - \lambda(|\Phi|^2 - v^2)^2 \\
&+ \frac{1}{16\pi G} R + g_\mathrm{wave} |\Phi|^2 R - \frac{g_\mathrm{wave}}{4} |\Phi|^2 (F^2 + G^2 + W^2) \\
&+ \bar{\psi}(i \not{D} - m)\psi + |\Phi|^2|H|^2,
\end{align}
where \(|\Phi|^2 = |\Phi_1|^2 + |\Phi_2|^2\), and \(g_\mathrm{wave}\) controls SBG. Fixed parameters: \(g_\mathrm{wave} \approx 19.5\), \(|\Phi_1| \approx 9.5 \times 10^{-4}\), \(|\Phi_2| \approx 0.5\), \(\varepsilon_\mathrm{CP} \approx 2.58 \times 10^{-41}\) \citep{BaldwinToE2025}.

\section{The Golden Spark and Early Density Patterning}
The Golden Spark at \( t \approx 10^{-36} \, \mathrm{s} \) splits the scalar field, seeding a density pattern:
\begin{equation}
\rho(r) = \rho_0 + \delta\rho \left[ |\Phi_1| \cos(k_\mathrm{wave} |r|) + |\Phi_2| \sin(k_\mathrm{wave} |r| + \varepsilon_\mathrm{CP} \pi) \right] \exp(-|r|/\lambda_d),
\end{equation}
with \(k_\mathrm{wave} \approx 2.35 \times 10^{-3}\) and \(\lambda_d = 1 \times 10^{20} \, \mathrm{m}\). This produces sharper crests than \(\Lambda\)CDM Gaussians, enabling early collapses without dark matter \citep{GoldenSpark2025}.

\section{Scalar Boosted Gravity (SBG) and Prompt Collapse}
SBG amplifies gravity via \(g_\mathrm{wave}|\Phi|^2\) in overdense phases, matching Bullet Cluster lensing without dark matter \citep{UWTBullet2025} and supporting rapid condensate formation.

\section{Scalar-Modified Black Hole Metric and Accretion}
A modified Schwarzschild metric:
\begin{equation}
ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{r_s}{r} + \varepsilon |\Phi_1 \Phi_2|^2\right) c^2 dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{r_s}{r} - \varepsilon |\Phi_1 \Phi_2|^2\right)^{-1} dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega^2,
\end{equation}
regularizes singularities and enables rapid, radiation-regulated accretion, consistent with JWST compactness \citep{BHUWT2025}.

Conventional seeding and growth scenarios struggle to explain these early supermassive black holes (SMBHs). We propose an alternative explanation based on Unified Wave Theory (UWT), a two-scalar-field framework with Scalar Boosted Gravity (SBG) emerging from a \emph{Golden Spark} phase splitting at \(t \approx 10^{-36}\)~s. This work supports a dark matter-free cosmology consistent with Bullet Cluster lensing and cosmological data, with testable predictions for JWST and LISA.

\section{Introduction}
Recent JWST observations \citep{Greene2023} have exposed surprisingly large SMBHs and compact galaxies at redshifts \(z > 10\), posing challenges to cold dark matter and hierarchical assembly scenarios. Standard models relying on Population III remnants or direct collapse seeds struggle to reach the observed black hole masses this early. In this study, we investigate a scalar field based alternative interfaced with gravitational effects from Unified Wave Theory (UWT) \citep{BaldwinToE2025}.

\section{The Unified Wave Theory Framework}
UWT posits that all fundamental forces and particle masses arise from two scalar fields \(\PhiOne\) and \(\PhiTwo\) defined on flat spacetime, governed by the Lagrangian:  
\[
L = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{a=1}^2 (\partial_{\mu} \Phi_a)^2 - \lambda (|\Phi|^2 - v^2)^2 + \frac{1}{16 \pi G} R + g_{\mathrm{wave}} |\Phi|^2 R + \cdots
\]
where \( |\Phi|^2 = |\PhiOne|^2 + |\PhiTwo|^2 \) and \(g_{\mathrm{wave}}\) controls Scalar Boosted Gravity \citep{BaldwinToE2025}. Key initial conditions are fixed by the ``Golden Spark'' event at \(t \approx 10^{-36}\) s, splitting the primordial scalar fields and imprinting density fluctuations.

\section{Golden Spark and Early Density Formation}
The Golden Spark seeds an initial overdensity pattern:
\[
\rho(r) = \rho_0 + \delta \rho \left[ |\PhiOne| \cos(k_{\mathrm{wave}} r) + |\PhiTwo| \sin(k_{\mathrm{wave}} r + \varepsilon_{\mathrm{CP}} \pi) \right] e^{-r/\lambda_d}
\]
with parameters calibrated from Planck 2016 data \citep{Planck2016}. This results in sharper crests than Gaussian fluctuations in \(\Lambda\)CDM, enabling prompt collapse without dark matter.

\section{Scalar Boosted Gravity and Black Hole Metric}
Scalar Boosted Gravity augments Newtonian gravity by a factor \(1 + g_{\mathrm{wave}} |\Phi|^2\), modifying gravitational lensing and accretion rates \citep{UWTBullet2025}. The Schwarzschild metric is modified as
\[
ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{r_s}{r} + \epsilon |\PhiOne \PhiTwo|^2\right) c^2 dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{r_s}{r} - \epsilon |\PhiOne \PhiTwo|^2\right)^{-1} dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega^2
\]
regularizing singularities and allowing radiation-regulated rapid SMBH growth consistent with JWST observations \citep{BHUWT2025}.

\section{Numerical Simulations}

\subsection{Simulation Setup}
We numerically evolve the scalar fields \(\PhiOne\) and \(\PhiTwo\) on a \(128^3\) cubic grid spanning \(10^{22}\) meters, incorporating gravitational feedback via iterative Poisson equation solving for scalar-boosted potential. Initial scalar states are seeded by Golden Spark conditions with enhanced multiscale perturbations for robustness. The time evolution uses RK45 with adaptive timestep \(dt = 500\) s over 2000 steps.
