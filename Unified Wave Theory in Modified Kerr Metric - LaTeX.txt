\section{Methodology}
The modified Kerr metric incorporates UWT's scalar fields \(\Phi_1\) and \(\Phi_2\) via
\begin{equation}
\Delta = r^2 - r_s r + \alpha^2 + g_{\text{wave}} \varepsilon |\Phi_1 \Phi_2|^2,
\end{equation}
with \(g_{\text{wave}} = 1 \times 10^{-6}\), \(\varepsilon = 10^{-30} \, \text{m}^2\), and \(|\Phi_1 \Phi_2|^2 \approx 2.256 \times 10^{-7}\), yielding \(\Delta \approx r^2 - r_s r + \alpha^2 + 2.256 \times 10^{-43} \, \text{m}^2\). The 2D slice at \(\theta = \pi/2\) is
\begin{equation}
ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{r_s}{r}\right) c^2 dt^2 + \frac{r^2}{\Delta} dr^2 + \left(r^2 + \alpha^2 + \frac{r_s \alpha^2}{r}\right) d\phi^2 - 2 \frac{r_s \alpha}{r} c dt d\phi.
\end{equation}

\section{Results}
Simulation results (steps 19000–22900) show:
- Max Velocity: 1.214 m/s to 1516 m/s,
- Divergence: 2268 to 22120, reduced to 2238.6 with AMR (256$^2$ grid, \(\nu = 10^{-4}\)),
- Enthalpy: \(2.709 \times 10^8\) to \(1.417 \times 10^9 \, \text{J/m}^3\).

\section{Discussion}
The entropy drop (\(\Delta S \approx -1.13 \times 10^6\) nats) and SBG stabilization (antigravity via \(\varepsilon |\Phi_1 \Phi_2|^2\)) support UWT. However, enthalpy exceeding \(10^8 \, \text{J/m}^3\) (reaching \(1.417 \times 10^9 \, \text{J/m}^3\)) suggests a potential for spacetime instability, such as micro-wormhole formation, though this remains speculative and requires further study. Caution is advised in high-energy regimes.
