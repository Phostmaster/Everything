\section{Introduction}
The Unified Wave Theory (UWT) refines the periodic table using the mass equation \(\langle m \rangle = \frac{\kappa A_f^3}{2 \lambda}\), with \(\kappa \approx \SI{5.06e-14}{\giga\electronvolt\squared}\), \(\lambda \approx \SI{2.51e-46}{1}\), and \(A_f = 2.84 \times 10^{-11} \cdot \frac{m_{\text{nucleon}} + E_b}{0.938} \cdot Z\), where \(m_{\text{nucleon}} \approx \SI{938}{\mega\electronvolt}\) and \(E_b\) is the binding energy. Quingal instability limits rows to 7.
