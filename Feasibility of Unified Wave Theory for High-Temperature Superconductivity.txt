\section{Introduction}
This paper explores the feasibility of the Unified Wave Theory (UWT), a two-field model \(\Phi = (\Phi_1, \Phi_2)\), in explaining high-temperature superconductivity through deviations in Higgs boson couplings. We present testable predictions based on recent analyses.

\section{Higgs Boson Couplings}
UWT introduces an effective potential modifying Higgs decay rates:
\begin{equation}
V_{eff} = V_h + \lambda_h |\Phi|^2 |h|^2,
\end{equation}
where \(\lambda_h \sim 10^{-3}\).

The decay rate for \(h \rightarrow \gamma\gamma\) is given by:
\begin{equation}
\Gamma(h \rightarrow \gamma\gamma) = \frac{\alpha^2 m_h^3}{256\pi^3 v^2} \left[ \sum_f N_c Q_f^2 A_{1/2}(\tau_f) + A_1(\tau_W) + \lambda_h \frac{|\Phi|^2}{m_h^2} \right]^2,
\end{equation}
with \(\alpha \approx \frac{1}{137}\), \(m_h \approx \SI{125}{\giga\electronvolt}\), \(v \approx \SI{246}{\giga\electronvolt}\), and \(|\Phi|^2 \approx \SI{0.0511}{\giga\electronvolt^2}\).

The UWT correction term is:
\begin{equation}
\lambda_h \frac{|\Phi|^2}{m_h^2} \approx 10^{-3} \cdot \frac{0.0511}{(125)^2} \approx 3.27 \times 10^{-6}.
\end{equation}

The Standard Model (SM) predicts \(\Gamma(h \rightarrow \gamma\gamma) \approx \SI{9.28}{\kilo\electronvolt}\). With UWT:
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_{UWT} \approx 9.28 \times (1 + 3.27 \times 10^{-6})^2 \approx \SI{9.28}{\kilo\electronvolt} \times 1.00000654.
\end{equation}
