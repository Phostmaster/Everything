\begin{abstract}
The Unified Wave Theory (UWT) enables neutrino-based faster-than-light (FTL) communication via scalar fields \(\Phi_1, \Phi_2\). A 1-meter laboratory test compares FTL signal propagation (~10\(^{-15}\) s) against light speed (3.33×10\(^{-9}\) s) using a Superconducting Quantum Interference Device (SQUID) and Bose-Einstein Condensate (BEC). The setup (0.12 m\(^3\), 0.382 J, 50 T) validates non-local signal transfer, aligning with UWT’s cosmological predictions (\(\delta T/T \approx 10^{-5}\)).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
UWT’s non-local scalar fields \(\Phi_1, \Phi_2\) enable FTL communication \cite{Weinberg1989}. This paper proposes a 1-meter lab test to confirm neutrino-based FTL signals.

\section{Theoretical Framework}
Neutrino FTL uses:
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
v_{\text{FTL}} &\propto g_{\text{wave}} \cdot |\Phi_1 \Phi_2|, \\
g_{\text{wave}} &\approx 19.5, \quad t_{\text{transit}} \approx 10^{-15} \, \text{s}.
\end{split}
\end{equation}
Parameters: \(\eta \approx 6 \times 10^{-10}\), \(\epsilon_{\text{CP}} \approx 2.58 \times 10^{-41}\).

\section{Experimental Setup}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{SQUID-BEC}: Rubidium-87 BEC (100 nK), SQUID (\(N = 10^6\), 10\(^{-6}\) m\(^2\)), 50 T.
    \item \textbf{Refrigerator}: 0.1 m\(^3\), 10 mK.
    \item \textbf{Vacuum Chamber}: 0.01 m\(^3\), 10\(^{-6}\) Pa.
    \item \textbf{Capacitors}: 0.01 m\(^3\), 0.382 J, 382 MW.
    \item \textbf{Detectors}: Neutrino source (670 nm laser), picosecond-precision at \(x = 0, 1 \, \text{m}\).
\end{itemize}

\section{Procedure}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Initialize: \(\Phi_1 = 0.00095\), \(\Phi_2 = 0.5\), \(\eta = 6 \times 10^{-10}\).
    \item Send neutrino signal at \(x = 0\), \(t = 0\).
    \item Measure: \(t_{\text{FTL}}\) vs. \(t_{\text{light}} = 3.33 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{s}\).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Expected Outcome}
Predicted: \(t_{\text{FTL}} \approx 10^{-15} \, \text{s}\), confirming non-local FTL.

\section{Conclusion}
The 1m test validates UWT’s neutrino FTL communication, paving the way for interplanetary applications.
