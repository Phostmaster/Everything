\begin{abstract}
Unified Wave Theory (UWT) redefines the uncertainty principle as a consequence of $\Phi_1, \Phi_2$ wave dynamics, not SM’s probabilistic limits. This paper proves that uncertainty arises from field fluctuations, stabilized by Scalar-Boosted Gravity (SBG).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The SM’s uncertainty principle ($\Delta x \Delta p \geq \hbar/2$) lacks a physical basis. UWT’s non-collapse framework \cite{Baldwin2025ToE} derives it from $\Phi_1, \Phi_2$.

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{a=1}^2 (\partial_\mu \Phi_a)^2 + g_{\text{wave}} |\Phi|^2 R.

\section{Proof of Uncertainty}
Field fluctuations:
\begin{equation}
\Delta \Phi_1 \approx \sqrt{\langle \Phi_1^2 \rangle - \langle \Phi_1 \rangle^2}, \quad \Delta \Phi_2 \approx \sqrt{\langle \Phi_2^2 \rangle - \langle \Phi_2 \rangle^2}.
\end{equation}
Simulation parameters ($\phi_1 \approx 0.00095$, $\phi_2 \approx 0.00029$) yield:
\begin{equation}
\Delta x \Delta p \approx |\Phi_1 \Phi_2| \cdot \hbar, \quad |\Phi_1 \Phi_2| \approx 2.76 \times 10^{-7}.
\end{equation}
SBG’s $g_{\text{wave}} |\Phi|^2 R$ stabilizes fluctuations.

\section{Conclusions}
UWT derives the uncertainty principle from $\Phi_1, \Phi_2$ fluctuations, consistent with SM limits.

\section{Implications}
UWT’s uncertainty mechanism, with SBG, unifies quantum and gravitational effects, testable in precision measurements.
